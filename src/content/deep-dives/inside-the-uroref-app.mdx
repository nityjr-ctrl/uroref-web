UroRef grew out of a simple frustration: useful information exists, but finding it quickly when you're on call is a different problem entirely.

Here's what's actually inside the app.

## On-Call Decision Trees

The emergency module is the heart of the app.

Each tree is built around the question you're actually asking at 2am — not a textbook walkthrough, but a binary decision path that gets you to the right action quickly.

<div class="thoughts-grid">
  <div class="thought-item">Obstructed infected kidney — resus, then decompress within hours.</div>
  <div class="thought-item">Priapism — ischaemic versus non-ischaemic pathway.</div>
  <div class="thought-item">Clot retention — three-way, CBI, theatre if it fails.</div>
  <div class="thought-item">Difficult catheterisation — step-by-step decision tree.</div>
</div>

Conditions covered include ureteric colic with obstruction, priapism, clot retention, haematuria, paraphimosis, testicular torsion, post-operative complications, and more.

Each tree ends with a clear action — not a differential list.

## Drug Doses

A curated reference for the drugs that actually come up in urology on call.

NSAIDs, alpha-blockers, antibiotics for urosepsis, intravesical agents, hormone therapy doses — with the specific context (indication, dose, route, contraindications) you need in the moment.

> No scrolling through BNF pages at 3am. The drugs are listed by clinical scenario, not alphabetically.

## Clinic Frameworks

Structured frameworks for outpatient urology — covering the common clinic presentations from new haematuria to lower urinary tract symptoms to post-operative follow-up.

These are particularly useful early in training when the structure of a urology consultation is still unfamiliar.

## 31 BAUS Patient Leaflets

The full suite of BAUS patient information leaflets, in-app and available offline.

Useful in clinic when a patient needs to take something away, or when you need to quickly check what patients are routinely told about a procedure before a consent conversation.

<p class="pullout">Everything in UroRef works offline. Because the wards, theatres and radiology corridors where you actually need this are the same places where hospital Wi-Fi reliably disappears.</p>

## Available Now

UroRef is live on iOS and Android, free for trainees. Over 500 UK urology trainees are already using it.
